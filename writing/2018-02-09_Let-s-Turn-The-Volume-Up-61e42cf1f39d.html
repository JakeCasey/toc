The idea is that we can talk about the high level stuff, where Storytime is going, what I want Storytime to do and how I want it to be done, and then I can explain some of the actual steps and things I’m taking to get it there. Sound cool? Cool.

Clouds

  • I want it to be simple. If it’s not simple, I won’t want to use it myself and I doubt anyone else will either. Normally when I’m about to listen to a podcast or some audio, I’m hopping in my car, or I’m about to start another task, or go to sleep. I’m impatient, and ain’t nobody got time to futz with apps that don’t work right the first time. If (when) I implement a login system, I want to be able to get my audio in… maybe 4-5 clicks or less. Preferably less.
  • I want the content to be high quality. I think the RSS feed options will be delicately curated. Part of the experience of the users of Storytime will be related to the actual content that they hear using the application. Typos for example are pretty evident in audio. I’ll have to build the feed list slowly and deliberately.
  • I recently decided that there’ll never be an app. Why? It’s complicated. I think the app ecosystem is detrimental and segregated. The Internet is an open and fair place, but as soon as you start writing code for Apple or Android, you’re pigeonholed into what they say you can and can’t do. I don’t like the feeling of that from a software design perspective or even just from any perspective. It gives me an ‘eeeeewwwww’ feeling. Now, that being said, there are some parts of a phone you can’t access from a web application. But for Storytime we can do everything we need from the web. I think.

Alright, here’s the deal, now I’m going to get into a little bit of the technical stuff. For those of you just here to see what I’m doing or what I’m building, I want people to have the option of just playing the audio that they just converted, straight from their phones. Why have to download the file at all, if you’re just going to play it right away? Why have to switch applications or import it to Itunes or whatever, if we can just play it straight away?
