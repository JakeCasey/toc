This is Why You Need To Make More Things

This post is only tangentially about Storytime. If you want to see where I am with that project, go check out this post right here. Else, let’s get on with the show!

I think there’s a massive lack of creation in today’s society. That sounds pretentious. Any sentence with the word ‘society’ in it sounds pretentious. Insert Tim Taylor grunt.

The point is, I think we’ve become people who almost solely consume. We consume content, media, resources. Eat food, toss away the wrapper, read the fancy buzzfeed-esque listicle (like this one, lol), but never use what you learned from it. Take, take, take. Everything we do these days, unless we’re actively involved in a hobby that lends itself to creation, is consumption. It’s extremely easy to fall into a rut, or to ‘go with the flow’ of every day, and never actually make anything.

Sure, you go to work, and maybe you cook something or make a graph or write a report and that’s technically creation. I think there’s something different that people are lacking. I think it’s creation for creation’s sake. ‘Creatives’ like musicians and artists of all kinds have this sensation of creation built into their chosen hobbies, but a lot of us don’t.

I, personally, have fallen into this trap. It’s easy to do, because under the guise of reading more books or articles or ‘learning’ more things, you never actually make anything. I need one more bit of knowledge before I can go make the thing.

It’s hard to explain the difference when you’re building things. I think it’s something very innately human, but when you’re building, it seems to lift this subtle malaise from the human experience. You can reach a state while building something that I compare to meditation. Especially when you’re not putting bets on any part of it — when you’re not attached to the outcome. You need to just create for the sake of creating.

1. You will be happier.

I can guarantee it, especially if you approach the act of creation with the right focus, just building something will make you happy. It’s a great feeling knowing you’ve built a thing. Now it’s out in the world. It’s there. A tiny mark you’ve made. Maybe somebody else can enjoy it, or use it, and that feels great.

2. You could make some $$$.

While I think survivorship bias is a big deal, even if you make just a few extra bucks a month from whatever you’ve built, that’s pretty cool. It’s important to remember that dollars are like… value tokens. If somebody is giving you their value tokens, it means that they valued what you’ve given them more than their value tokens.

3. You will learn something.

Until now we’ve been talking in ambiguous terms of stuff. And things. And somethings. And thingies. I’ve told you why you should build, but what can I build? It’s easy. Anything.

  • Write an article like this one, but about something you know better than anyone else. Do you knit? Write a short post about your favorite needles, or thread or technique. Remember, it doesn’t matter what other people think of it. We’re creating something just for the act of creation.
  • There’s an insane amount of information on pretty much any topic you can think of on the internet. Believe it or not, it’s not just cat memes and funny videos out there on the internet. You could learn cardistry and make a cool video like this:

You could learn how to sculpt something, which would be pretty cool:

You could learn to code (and build things!) at Free Code Camp

Maybe you want to learn guitar and write a song?

What I’m telling you is that right now there has never been a better time to learn something. Anything. And because you can learn anything, you can create a lot of things, too, and I think creating, and leaving a lasting mark on the world, no matter how minimal, is an an amazing way to live your life.

If you’re creating something, if you’re working on something, I want to hear about it. How can I help?
