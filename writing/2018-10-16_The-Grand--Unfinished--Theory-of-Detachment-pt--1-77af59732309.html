The Grand (Unfinished) Theory of Detachment pt. 1

The Grand Theory of Detachment is this: “The less you’re attached to the outcome of anything, the more likely that the outcome will be in your favor.”

10 minutes is all I have to tell you this. Because I have to go to work and it’s early and I haven’t had any coffee. And that’s ok. 10 minutes isn’t a very long time. Especially if you enjoy thinking about what you’re writing. There’s a certain nuance to crafting sentences and ideas and putting them down on the page. There’s a nuance and a craft to not putting ideas and sentences down, too. You can say you hate writing (me) or say you don’t have time (also me). These are carefully thought out ideas. Designed and developed to avoid pressing keys on the keyboard. I’ve built them up after past writing failures.

Tesla is up today, my phone just told me.

I think this is the second or third time I’ve announced my triumphant return to writing. I’m back bitches, again. I think for anyone who’s done any amount of writing, you’ll understand. Writing is a toxic relationship. It’s the most beautiful woman, she’ll caress and hold you and your friends will say ‘Wow, you’re really lucky!’ and then occasionally she’ll kick you in the face. But you still love her. She’s beautiful and attractive and she makes you feel whole. But she still tells you you suck and that you should quit trying so hard.

I started with a title that makes this seem much more important than it really is. Holy shit, I only have two minutes left. What’s the point? I spent my time meditating today not meditating. I was actually agonizing about what I was going to write in these 10 minutes. This writing woman has got me so torn up inside. Invasive and obsessive thoughts, like a jealous lover.

Oops. Chimes. Time’s up. I’m glad I didn’t set the same alarm as I use for waking up, because that shit gives me heart issues. Klaxon. Danger. Time to go to work.

Love you, bye.
