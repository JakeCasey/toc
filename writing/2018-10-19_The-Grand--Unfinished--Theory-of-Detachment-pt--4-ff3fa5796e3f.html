The Grand (Unfinished) Theory of Detachment pt. 4

If it’s any indication of my massive shift in my writing habit, I’ve started this *the night before* I’m supposed to. Levels posted this tweet a day or two ago:

And I’m gonna talk about it. Tomorrow morning.

Whew, good morning, lovelies. Ten minutes locked and loaded.

Close to a few months ago I set up Telegram and joined a group called Work In Progress I can’t actually tell you how I got there or even how I found it in the first place. Maybe WIP found me. I don’t know. WIP is a group for entrepreneurs, software engineers and designers.

We all have secret inner worlds. Full of opinions and interest and habits and even their own lexicons. These inner worlds are the things we think about every day. They are filled with our own personal intense interests. And these inner worlds are being smothered. It feels like being suffocated.

Our friend group is typically limited by our general social interactions, and our actual physical location. The people we grow up with, the people we meet at school or work, whatever.

I think as we grow older we all develop our own preferences and our own thoughts and habits and interests, and as we age, those ‘inner worlds’ can diverge from our friend group. It happens slowly, and we may barely notice it. But socially we don’t just say ‘I’m done’ with those people, because that’s mean and obviously they’re our friends.

But maybe they’re not really into the same things we are. Maybe they don’t give a fuck about code or entrepreneurship. Or painting. Or music. Or what’s going on at your job. Of course they’ll ask about it and talk about it and they’ll likely have some genuine interest.

But I think at some point we all get a little lost. Maybe even a little lonely. Do our inner worlds really overlap with our friends? We know that we love the people around us, but maybe they’re not exactly the perfect fit. And I don’t know that they need to be.

This all sounds very cynical and that’s really not how I mean it to come out.

What I’m trying to get at, is there are niche groups of people who’s interests align nearly exactly with your own. It’s those types of people where it’s possible to derive the peak amount of social satisfaction from. Lengthy debates, opinionated forums, and help and support in our turbulent and emotional worlds.

If you are a fly fishing aficionado, you need to find a group of fly fishermen to talk to.

You gotta get your thoughts and feelings about that passionate something off your chest. You need to get them heard by other passionate fly fishermen.

You need to find your niche. Whatever it is that you’re into. You need to search them out. I’m not saying get rid of your old friends, that’s not what I mean. They’re still great and they’ll still love you, I guarantee it. The cool thing about the internet is that you can get to these communities. For me, WIP is an amazing escape. Somewhere where everybody knows what MRR is. 🎶 Where everybody knows your name. 🎶 Where I can ask questions about projects I’m working on openly and freely.

I love my best friend, but if I were to go up to him and say “What do you think the conversion rate on this landing page is gonna be?” He’s going to look at me like a crazy person.

And so when I send some memes to WIP, I feel just a little more included. Just a little more like I belong. Belonging. That’s important, and easily overlooked.

You need to find your WIP.

Til next time,

Love you, bye.
