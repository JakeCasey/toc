Do you beat yourself up?

I do.

It’s been two weeks since I’ve written anything. I’m lazy. I’m useless. I can’t commit to anything — woah, woah.

Hold up.

I’m just as hard on myself as all of you, but here’s the deal. Sometimes you must be easy on yourself. In the game of life consistency and volume is key. And sometimes you’ll be inconsistent.

Sometimes you’ll be lazy and say “Not tonight, I think I’m gonna have a beer instead.”

Just like when I stopped writing for two weeks. Ha ha ha…

But I’ve lowered my goal (so it seems less daunting to help with adherence), and I’ve thought about what and why I want to write.

All these things will hopefully help me to do it again, yay!

So what have I been up to? Well, I’ve installed Dokku on my servers. Dokku is a PaaS that you host on your own server. The way that I like to think about it is it’s one or two days of nightmare dev ops to never have to worry about dev ops again. Which I think is going to help me with my next goal:

To finish one side project every month for the remainder of the year.

I was struggling with topics to write about. Which is a weak ass excuse. I figured the easiest way to have something to write about is to do something interesting. Setting up Dokku was the first step for that.

It was rough. Tell you hwhat. While not particularly weak in dev ops sometimes pushing and pulling and doing all of the stuff required to get a server up and running is a NIGHTMARE. Tiny little differences in tons of places need to all be mystically aligned. You need to sacrifice a goat and the moon needs to be in the right place in the sky.

And then your server spins up and you have an application.

Dokku relieves some of that mysticism and folly.

Typically an application results from a properly worded git push dokku. It’s like white magic and black magic. You gotta say the correct words (levi-oh-sah) and suddenly you get a web app. It’s nifty, and I like it.

Anyway, the first thing I did was get an up and running version of Skeleton Key. Sick. It lives. I can do things. I can write and deploy applications quickly and efficiently. That was the goal of Dokku and Skeleton Key. To be able to build apps faster.

Second, I started working on Project #9 Body of Knowledge.

I like naming things dramatically. But I’ll talk about the Body of Knowledge tomorrow. ❤ Also if you haven’t played the game Apex Legends it just came out, definitely give it a shot.
