1 mindset change that will transform how you travel for cheap.

tl dr; Don’t spend so much money.

Wrapping up the first few days (actually closer to a week!) of our three week trip has me thinking, as travel often does. It gets the gears turning, and makes you think a little harder about something as simple as what to eat for dinner. It’s not an automatic response. Situations like ‘Where’s the bathroom?’ become. . . entertaining. It’s one of the reasons I adore traveling, the total lack of routine. It breaks me out of the daily drudgery that seems to make days pass so fast, and is so easy to slip into.

There’s a connection between travel, meditation and being ‘present minded’.

But that isn’t what this post is about, it’s about traveling on the cheap.

Controlled vs Uncontrolled

For lack of better terms I classify everything I spend on a trip between controllable and uncontrollable expenses. I get my uncontrollables from inter-country travel, and big purchases that are unavoidable. Uncontrollable expenses are plane tickets and museum passes, things that are must do (for me). Your uncontrollables might be different. You could focus on eating out, drinking, whatever. I keep them separate from my total because it’s not good to lump them in with everything else. Controlled expenses are the things you have control of. Where you eat, what you eat, where you stay, how much you drink. The controlled expenses are the only ones that matter to me, but for full disclosure my uncontrolled expenses thus far are as follows:

ORD -> JFK : 69 USD

JFK -> CDG (Paris) : 225 USD

ORY -> FCO (Rome) : 178 USD

Paris Pass : 60 USD

532 USD

As far as attempting to keep uncontrollables low, check out Hitlist and Momondo. I’ve also heard good things about Skiplagged, but have never used it. Searching for cheap flights on the internet has never been easier, so no excuses. The Paris Pass was a no brainer, as it was 60 bucks that got us into various Museums and areas around Paris for 4 days. Entry to the Louvre alone is 15 euro.

Let me hit you with some more numbers.

New York City, New York : 149.25 USD

Paris, France : 194.41 USD

That’s what I’ve spent in controlled expenses in the two cities, thus far. Though I haven’t left Paris quite yet. Huge shoutout to St. Christopher’s Inn. We’re only going to be staying one night, but I can already tell it’s nicer than where we’ve been for the past few days. Now some dates for context.

New York City, New York : Tuesday, July 11th approx 9AM — Thursday, July 13th 11:55PM

Paris, France : Friday, July 14th 1pm — Tuesday, July 18th 3pm

For New York let’s call it 3 days, and for Paris let’s call it 4. That puts our average for NYC at 49.75 a day, and for Paris currently I’m at 48.60/day. This number is gonna go up because we’re gonna go out for lunch tomorrow and have a few beers tonight.

How can you have any fun on 50 dollars a day?

It’s a mindset change for most people that’s for sure. Everyone who knows me knows that I’m pretty cheap. I pretty much spend money on beer and food because those are the things I enjoy. I live a pretty good life on the amount of money that I spend. At home this mindset translates to salads, steaks and chicken. Yes. Cooking at home is cheaper than eating out. You should try it. But abroad it’s a little different. It’s an adventure. Most hostels (except our last one, jesus.) provide a kitchen for cooking in. That means you can take an hour out of your busy travel schedule, go to the local market, pick up some food and cook it. Let’s say you eat out twice a day, at 20 euro a pop, 40 euro is close to enough for two dorm beds at a lot of hostels around Europe. That’s an insane amount of money.

Ok, Jake. All you’ve told me really is ‘spend less money’, where’s the real travel hack?

That is the real travel hack. Sleep in hostels (they’re not like the movie, usually) or airBNB. Eat a little less food, you don’t need as much as you think you do. Don’t eat out every meal. Reuse your water bottle. Walk more. Think hard and use the internet to find great things to do for free or cheap.

Worth it? It depends on your priorities and your focus. If sampling the cuisine of the country you’re in is one of your main focuses then by all means. Don’t get me wrong though, you can still get a taste of the town without spending an arm and a leg. Street food is an excellent example. In some of the less touristic parts of Paris we’ve seen crepes for 2 euro. A great cultural snack that doesn’t eat your budget.

As far as can you have fun on ~50 dollars a day?

I don’t know. Seems fun to me. Save some money. Get out and do it.
