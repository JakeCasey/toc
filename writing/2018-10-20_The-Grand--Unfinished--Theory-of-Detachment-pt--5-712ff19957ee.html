The Grand (Unfinished) Theory of Detachment pt. 5

Good Morning! I don’t have much time today, so let’s get to it.

The water was nice and cool. I remember that. It’s always hot in Vietnam. At least it felt like it. Unless it was a particularly rainy day, it was always hot. Sometimes oppressively so. It was a thick fog of heat that would settle on you and stick with you all day. Of course, by this time, it was two or three in the morning. So it had cooled off considerably. My heartbeat was racing, and I’m not gonna lie, I was pretty drunk. As one does when it’s extremely late, some friends from the hostel I worked at had pulled me aside at the end of my shift, and in hushed tones asked “Wanna go swimming?”

And now I found myself illicitly splish-splashing in an outdoor hotel pool not far from the hostel I was working in in Hue, Vietnam. Dimly lit, we crept and biked through several back alleys until we reached a nondescript brick wall. In several furtive movements we scrambled up the sides, and landed on the other side. The four of us, two girls, and two guys, stripped to our undergarments and hopped in!

It was glorious. It was the perfect crime. I mean that in more ways than one.

And so we sat for blissful minutes, chatting in quiet tones.

And a shock of white light lit up the hotel pool. Awash in our newfound exposure, we all exited the pool, in a panic. “Run!” someone whisper-shouted. Which I always found an interesting experience. The only time someone ever whispers that urgently is pretty much to shout ‘run’.

And so after grabbing my things and throwing them over the wall, I then threw myself over the wall. There’s where things got a little dicey. Being drunk, and a little less than coordinated even whilst sober, I landed from a good seven or eight foot drop, only on my right heel. It hurt like a sonofabitch. I hobbled quickly behind my friends, bringing up the rear, in a panicked and painful shuffle.

I’m fairly certain I had broken my heel. I couldn’t walk on it for days. It was worth days of pain. Just for the story.

Time’s up. I’ll finish this story someday.

Love you, bye.
