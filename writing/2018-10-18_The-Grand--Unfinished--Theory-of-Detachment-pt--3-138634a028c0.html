The Grand (Unfinished) Theory of Detachment pt. 3

Good Morning. Today there’s something I’d like to talk about: My road before learning to code. I am a self-taught developer, with a nice fun remote Wordpress dev job. But I wasn’t always. Who was, really?

Stick with me for a moment. We’re going to go on a little adventure. This is my magical Time Machine. Yes, I know it looks like a cardboard box. It’s because cardboard boxes are never out of place. Unless you go back far enough, I guess. What year was corrugated cardboard invented? Whatever. Twist some knobs here, pull this lever there, voilá. No, I swear it’s safe. When will we get back? I have no idea. Probably around now. Go ahead, step inside. Mind your elbows. Going back to 2009 in those shoes? Your call. Beep Boop. We’re off, soaring through the rivers of time!

And with a rattle and hum, we’re back. It’s 2009. I’m more of a child than I am now. Literally, not figuratively. Well, both, I suppose. Times are turbulent. I’m much more emotional. I have intense feelings about people and things. I have just read Tim Ferris’ “Four Hour Workweek” and my future education is looking bleak. I reluctantly sign up for community college. Network Administration. I have not left the country yet. I’ve spent pretty much the entirety of my life on computers thus far. But only superficially. I know enough to troubleshoot and fix problems. I’m the ‘computer guy’ of the family. I know basic HTML and CSS. I hate switches and telnet.

Community college for some reason just spurns me. I hate it. I hate every second of it. It grinds on me mentally and defeats me. Looking back, I realize I should’ve just finished my degree. Some people wear their not finishing school as a badge of honor. I particularly do not. I was weak to not finish. I shouldn’t have let how I felt about schooling change that outcome.

But what’s done is done. Let’s skip ahead a bit, this part is full of the woe is me late teen shit. Back in the machine, quick step, please. This thing makes more noise every time I start it up. Sounds like a chainsaw doesn’t it?

Now we arrive a close to two years later! Va-voom! I quit school, and eventually moved out. I worked two jobs. I delivered sandwiches for a popular scary quick™ sandwich delivery restaurant, and I worked as a night manager at a local hotel. How they decided that would be a good idea is beyond me. I was liked by the actual management, and so I think it passed. Somehow. Tim Ferris still lurks in the back of my mind like some type of weird muse (ya’ll who’ve read it will get that joke).

Disclaimer: These are not actual Tim Ferriss quotes. They’re just what the Tim in my head was saying at the time. They’re mostly accurate though.

He skulked and poked holes in my thoughts. “Why get a desk job? You’ll lose your mind.”, “Sometimes it’s better to live life on your own terms.”, “Get excited” and most importantly,

“You should go to Thailand.”

So I went. By myself. I meant to leave for two months but ended up staying close to eight.

Back in the Time Machine. We can go back and visit Thailand later, but I’ll sum it up here real quick. It broke me from ever having a normal life. At least for the foreseeable future. Past. From then until now, I haven’t had a particularly normal go at it. There we go. It also convinced me that the only option in my life was to start my own gig.

And to do that I needed to learn how to code.

So I came home from London — maybe I’ll explain how I ended up there another time — and opened Code Academy on my laptop. After all, with the internet and a shitty Chrome Book, I could take over the world, right?

Til next time,

Love you, bye.
