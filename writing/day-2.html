Day # 2

I have a hard time with selfless creation.

I think it's important.

I think people should be building things to give value to the betterment of society. I struggle with that idea sometimes when building things. How to take value-- how can I make money-- seeps into the things we do. It's some creeping fungus that poisons budding good intentions. Trying to squeeze every dollar out of your customer is needful, it is 'taking', it is 'scarcity'. I think taking value and scarcity are what ultimately causes many businesses and entrepreneurs to fail.

Goddamnit, I want to make a living doing my own thing though. Is that so wrong?

So, I suppose, here are some words on being genuine and why sometimes fake it til you make it isn't the play.

First, and foremost, I want to mention that I just think this is the best way of success. Not the only way. You CAN fake it til you make it. You CAN have no skin in the game and still build a massive company. But it's harder, and here's why.

The best work comes from a place of joy.

I think it comes from somebody just saying "Fuck it let's build this weird thing."

Or from passion, "The world absolutely needs this and I'm going to build it whether or not somebody ever uses it, because it needs to exist."

These are genuine feelings. Real, honest, true feelings. Genuine thoughts, feelings, and motivations are what, I believe, helps some entrepreneurs succeed. There is no layer of ambiguity separating the creator and the consumer of the product.

When you're creating from this genuine place, it's all on the table. There's no pretending that the exchange of money and services isn't what it is. The interaction can move smoothly and beautifully.

Customers, like wild animals, can smell fear. But customers, unlike wild animals, can also smell disingenuousness. They can tell when a creator isn't giving. When a creator isn't really trying to give the value they want to give. There's a catch. They're trying to take. This is what holds a lot of us back from success. Myself included.

So, ultimately, how do we combat it? Even in ourselves, as entrepreneurs of all types, how do we stop the poison from creeping into our products and the things that we do?

The easiest way is to have fun and be honest. If you're having fun building your product, real fun, it shields your work from this disingenuousness. You should do weird and whacky shit because you want to. You should build a few things just to see how they turn out, not expecting to make money at all. I think you should enjoy the work that you put into them, however you have to justify it to yourself to do so. Fun helps you be a little less outwardly validated, and that's big.

And I think we need to be more honest, with ourselves and with the people who use our products. Perhaps you're not passionate about the vertical you're in. That's ok, as long as you're passionate about something else in the fulfillment of that vertical. Let's say you sell washing machines. Perhaps you don't like washing machines, but you love the art and craft of selling them. Or you love the engineering of them. Whatever.

You don't have to love the whole package. But you absolutely must be honest with yourself and everyone else where you stand in regards to your product. The one thing you cannot do is pretend. About pretty much anything. Be genuine. Be you. Be a struggling entrepreneur who comes from low-income background. Or be the struggling entrepreneur who comes from a rich family, it doesn't matter. But you have to be that, not anything else.
